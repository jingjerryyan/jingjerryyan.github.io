My Chinese Name: 闫璟 (Yán Jǐng)

This page offers a brief introduction to my name and story. I go by Jing (Jerry) in Hong Kong and the U.S., though I was born and raised in Hohhot, Inner Mongolia. My Chinese name often poses pronunciation challenges for non-Mandarin speakers, and this guide explains its meaning.

My Name

闫 (Yán) surname

  • Pronunciation: IPA: [jɛn]; "yén" – akin to "yen" currency, lips spread as in "yes", high-flat pitch.
  • Original meaning: "Gatekeeper; custodian of inner gates"
  • Components: Outer: 门 (gate); Inner: 三 (symbolizing multiple gates). Together depicting successive gateways guarding a precinct.

璟 (Jǐng) first name

  • Pronunciation: IPA: [tɕiŋ]; "jǐng" – soft "j" as in "jeep" followed by nasal "-ing", rising then falling pitch.
  • Original meaning: "The inner luminosity of flawless jade"
  • Components: Left: 王 (jade radical); Right: 景 (scenery/illumination). Together evoking the play of light across precious stone.

This page offers a brief introduction to my name and story. I go by Jing (Jerry) in Hong Kong and the U.S., though I was born and raised in Hohhot, Inner Mongolia. My Chinese name — 闫璟 (Yán Jǐng) — often poses pronunciation challenges for non-Mandarin speakers, so what follows explains how it sounds and what it means.

The Name

闫 (Yán) · surname

Pronunciation IPA [jɛn]; “yén” — akin to “yen” currency, lips spread as in “yes,” high, flat pitch.

Meaning Gatekeeper; custodian of inner gates.

Components Outer 门 (gate) enclosing inner 三 (successive gates) — depicting gateways that guard a precinct.

璟 (Jǐng) · given name

Pronunciation IPA [tɕiŋ]; “jǐng” — soft “j” as in “jeep” followed by a nasal “-ing,” rising then falling pitch.

Meaning The inner luminosity of flawless jade.

Components Left 王 (the jade radical) beside right 景 (scenery / illumination) — evoking the play of light across a precious stone.

Put together, 闫璟 reads as light kept safe behind successive gates — a quiet luminosity, guarded and enduring.
